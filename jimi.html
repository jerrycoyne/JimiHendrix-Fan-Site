The Greatest of All Time in only 27 Years

James Marshall "Jimi" Hendrix (born Johnny Allen Hendrix; November 27, 1942 – September 18, 1970) was an American rock guitarist, singer, and songwriter. Although his mainstream career spanned only four years, he is widely regarded as one of the most influential electric guitarists in the history of popular music, and one of the most celebrated musicians of the 20th century. The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame describes him as "arguably the greatest instrumentalist in the history of rock music". Born in Seattle, Washington, Hendrix began playing guitar at the age of 15. He then played with Curtis Knight and the Squires before moving to England in late 1966 after being discovered by Linda Keith, who in turn interested bassist Chas Chandler of the Animals in becoming his first manager. Within months, Hendrix had earned three UK top ten hits with the Jimi Hendrix Experience: "Hey Joe", "Purple Haze", and "The Wind Cries Mary". He achieved fame in the U.S. after his performance at the Monterey Pop Festival in 1967, and in 1968 his third and final studio album, Electric Ladyland, reached number one in the U.S.; it was Hendrix's most commercially successful release and his first and only number one album. The world's highest-paid performer, he headlined the Woodstock Festival in 1969 and the Isle of Wight Festival in 1970, before his accidental death from barbiturate-related asphyxia on September 18, 1970, at the age of 27. (wikipedia)

The Music

Jimi's career start date is widely debated. He was active as a musician as early as 1962, However he released his first studio album Are You Experienced on August 23rd, 1967. It peaked the US charts at #5 but went on to be 5 times Multi Platinum with hits like "Purple Haze", "Hey Joe", "The Wind Cries Mary" and even more. In 1967 he released Axis: Bold as Love(Album Cover featured in this section). Axis: Bold as Love further solidified Hendrix as a staple of psychadelic rock. This album featured short songs, but what he lacked in length he made up for in quality. Songs like "Little Wing" and "Castles Made of Sand" helped push this album up to #3 on the US charts which achieved Platinum. In October of 1968 Hendrix once again took the world by storm with Electric Ladyland. This album busted out on the scene with "Voodoo Child", "Crosstown Traffic", and a cover of Bob Dylan's "All Along the Watchtower". This was the peak of Jimi Hendrix and the charts showed it, Electric Ladyland hit #1 on the US Charts. It also went on to be 2 times Platinum.

The Legacy

Almost 50 years after Jimi's early passing his legacy still lives on. His unique "crying" playing style to this day is a highly saught after sound in the music world. His sound has been tried to be replicated and monitzed by many music equipment companies such as Dunlop, who has an effect pedal named the "Cry Baby" after Hendrix's unbelievable sound. Along with his musical legacy he also carried a civil rights legacy. Before the Civil rights act of 1964, Jimi was a house hold name and well regarded as a respected musician in a time where blacks and whites were still segregated in many aspects of life. Many publications including Rolling Stone Magazine have named him the greatest guitarist of all time.
